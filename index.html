Welcome!

My name is Maximilian Fuchs, I am a PhD candidate in financial economics at Copenhagen Business School and a research fellow at the European Central Bank. I have professional experience in banking, insurance, and research.

My research interests lie at the intersection of financial markets and climate change with applications to asset pricing and macroprudential policy.

Feel free to reach out!

Polluters are Short-Lived: Climate Risk and the Timing of Cash Flows
I construct a measure of cash flow duration at the firm level and link it to carbon emissions of the same firm.

Climate Policy Action and the Pricing of Bank Loans
Using granular credit register data of Euro area banks, information on borrowers’ greenhouse gas emissions and their countries’ degree of climate action, we study whether banks price climate-related policy risk in their corporate loan portfolios.
